Biography

The Early Years
in West Bengal

Born in the village of Aiho, West Bengal, Professor Momtazuddin Ahmed's life was a testament to the power of the written word. While official documents note his birth as January 18, 1935, family lore recalls a winter afternoon in 1933.

His early education was shaped by the poems taught by his elder sister, Anju Bubu, and a rigorous academic upbringing at Malda District School.

Development

Education and
Identity

The Partition of 1947 forced a relocation to Bholahat (now Bangladesh). Completing his matriculation amidst political shifts, he emerged as a voice for the Bengali identity.

His transition into the world of drama allowed him to dissect the human condition, eventually becoming one of the most respected playwrights in South Asian history.
